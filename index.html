The Place

Eight centuries
of pilgrimage

The hermitage of São Pedro de Vira-a-Corça sits at the foot of Monsanto hill — Mons Sanctus, the sacred mountain. Its door opens through a semicircular arch crowned by a rose window; inside, a tripartite nave leads to an apse flanked by narrow chapels.

In front of the chapel, perched on a bare granite boulder, stands a simple bell tower that seems to defy gravity — a fitting sentinel for a place that has drawn pilgrims since ancient times.

1200s The temple is built in the early 13th century at the foot of Monsanto hill.
1308 King Dinis grants a Fair Charter to the annual fair held beside the hermitage.
1400s The hermitage is renovated, taking the form pilgrims still see today.
2026 On October 17, the ancient pilgrimage site joins a planet-wide moment of connection.
